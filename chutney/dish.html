How to use leftover chutney

How to use leftover chutney

Wondering what to do with the rest of that jar of chutney that's sitting in the fridge? Here are some tasty ways to make sure you don't waste that delicious, tangy chutney

Chutney and Cream Cheese Spread

Place a block of cream cheese on a plate and spoon the chutney of your choice over it. Serve with crackers and a small spreading knife.

Chutney and Grilled Cheese Sandwiches

Spread a layer of chutney on a slice of bread. Top with thin slices of cheese. A tangy cheese such as a cheddar is a good choice. Then layer silces of onion and tomato. Broil the sandwich until the cheese starts to bubble. Serve hot.

Chutney Roasted with Veggies/ Panner/ Meat

Preheat the oven to 400F/200C. Chop mixed veggies like broccoli, onion, mushroom and zucchini. You can also add 1-inch chunks of Panner(or meat, but cooking time will be longer). Spread a sheet of aluminium foil in a roasting pan. In a large bowl, combine the veggies with chutney, 2 tablespoons olive oil and salt as taste. (Use about 1/4-cup chutney for every 2 pounds of vegetables). Transfer to the roasting pan and spread into an even layer. Bake for about 15 to 20 minutes, until tender but slightly crispy. Enjoy!

Chutney and Chicken

Marinate some cubed chicken breasts with chutney for about 15 to 20 minutes. Saute some onions in a olive oil and then add the chicken. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 10 mins, stirring occasionally. Then add a heaping tablespoon of tomato paste with just a dash of water to liquify. Stirr and continue to cook (covered) for another 10 mins until cooked through. Delicious when served with rice!

There are so many more wonderful ways you can use chutney with your food. The possibilities are endless.
